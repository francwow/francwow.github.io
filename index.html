Tennis & Running

I started playing tennis at a very early age. Growing up I dreamed of becoming a succesful and recognized tennis player. As I grew older I also wanted different things, relationships and carreer paths. Suddenly I opened my mind to other possibilities of making a living, but still, tennis remained a core part of my being as a way of competing and building mental strength. In my teenage years, I was never really exited about the idea of going for a run or trying to improve my sprinting time. I guess as I became more mature and time bacame more valuable in my life, I naturally gravitated towards ways of achieving physical exaustion through more practical and efficient ways. I remember the first time I decided to go for a propper "run", I put on my tennis shoes, asseembled a "cool" playlist on my phone and walked to the nearest park. I started jogging slowly, 15 minutes felt like forever and by the time I stopped due to pain on my feet it was raining. But damn it felt so good, better than any other thing I felt before. Needless to say I bacame kind of addicted to it, running gives you a certain high when you do it for a while, it feels like nothing matters in the universe, not even you.
